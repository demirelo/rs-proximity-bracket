## 1. Conventions

**The ceiling convention ($R_{\mathrm{cap}}$, load-bearing).** Two nearby radii appear in the literature and in our tooling: the **exact inverse-entropy crossing** $R_{\mathrm{cap}}(q,\rho):=H_q^{-1}(1-\rho)$ — the radius $\delta$ solving $H_q(\delta)=1-\rho$, the convention of ledger §3b / `calculator/proximity_parameters.py` — and the **large-$q$ approximation** $1-H_q(\rho)$. **Every proven upper-ceiling statement in this ledger is a statement about $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$;** the large-$q$ approximation appears only where explicitly labeled as such. The two are **not equal at any deployed rate** (verified by direct computation): the gap is $\le0.0017$ across deployed rates and field sizes (maximum $\approx0.00166$ at 31-bit, $\rho=\tfrac18$), with rate-dependent sign — the approximation sits *below* $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ at $\rho=\tfrac12$ (by $+9.6\cdot10^{-5}$ at 31-bit, $+1.7\cdot10^{-7}$ at 256-bit) and *above* it at $\rho\le\tfrac14$ (by $\approx2.0$–$2.3\cdot10^{-5}$ at 256-bit) — i.e. the two differ **beyond the fifth decimal** at some rates even at 256-bit, and the approximation can overstate the proven ceiling, which is exactly why $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ is the proven object. The large-$q$ approximation sits a distance $H_q(\rho)-\rho\le1/\log_2 q$ below Singleton capacity (CS25 Claim 1; $\le1/31$ for 31-bit fields).

**A historical software caveat — now FIXED (R29/M2 follow-up).** The resolver `calculator/listsize_resolution.py` formerly capped the alphabet at `_QCAP_LOG2 = 200` before evaluating $H_q$, so its generated "256-bit" ceiling entries were in fact **200-bit** values — e.g. $0.49500$ at $\rho=\tfrac12$ vs the true 256-bit large-$q$-approximation value $0.49609$. The cap is now $320$ (no deployed field is capped) and the resolver reports the proven ceiling at the field's true $q$; all 256-bit ceiling values quoted in this ledger are true $q=2^{256}$ values, matching `problem-ledger.md` §3b and the regenerated `listsize-analysis.md` §8. The exact 256-bit crossings $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ are $0.49609/0.74681/0.87285/0.93616$ at $\rho=\tfrac12/\tfrac14/\tfrac18/\tfrac1{16}$ (the large-$q$ approximation would give $0.49609/0.74683/0.87288/0.93618$).

**The notation clash (carry the ABF convention).** ABF/CS25/GG25 use $\delta$ for the proximity *radius*; **BCHKS and CGHLL use $\delta$ for the code's minimum distance** ($=1-\rho$, MDS) and $\gamma$ (resp. $\theta$) for the radius. BCHKS's soundness error is $a/q$ where $a=\#\{z:\Delta(f+zg,C)\le\gamma\}$ and $q=|F|$; its "proximity loss" is $\varepsilon^\ast$ (the gap between hypothesis and conclusion radii). When importing BCHKS into ABF: radius $\delta\equiv\gamma$, rate $\rho\equiv1-\delta_{\text{BCHKS}}$, error $a/q$ with $a$ a count. **CS25's "capacity" is the *list-decoding* capacity** (our $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$, which they state via the large-$q$ approximation), strictly below the Singleton $1-\rho$ that ABF/BCHKS/GG call capacity. **CGHLL's "Elias radius" is likewise the large-$q$ approximation** $1-H_q(\rho)$; wherever we quote their conjectures we write $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$, the difference ($\le0.0017$) being below the resolution of every claim involved. All statements below are in the ABF convention.

## 2. The MCA bracket in detail (sub-problem 1)

This section records the *bracket* $[\delta_{\text{known-positive}},\,\delta_{\text{known-negative}}]$ for the MCA challenge; the true $\delta^\ast_C$ lies inside it, and pinning it is open. **This is a bracket, not a theorem-complete determination.** Scoping rule (carried from the short paper §5, load-bearing): the known bracket *endpoints* are field-type insensitive; the negative *mechanism* is field-agnostic (§3.2); the *positive* beyond-Johnson program is **not** field-agnostic (§3.5).

### 2.1 Positive side — up to the Johnson radius (all fields)

**[PROVEN] (Bordage–Chiesa–Guan–Manzur, eprint 2025/2051, Thm. 9.2).** For $C=\mathrm{RS}[F,D,k]$ over *any* domain $D$ (including smooth subgroups), the affine-line / univariate-powers generator, message degree $d=k-1$, and integer $m\ge3$:
$$
\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}(C,\delta)\;\le\;\frac{(m+\tfrac12)^7\,n^2\,d}{3\,\rho^{3/2}\,|F|},\qquad\text{valid for }\delta\le1-\big(1+\tfrac1{2m}\big)\sqrt\rho.
$$
Loss-free (= ABF $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}$); as $m\to\infty$ the validity window $\to J=1-\sqrt\rho$, but larger $m$ inflates the error via $(m+\tfrac12)^7$ (the $m$-vs-$\delta$ trade).

**[PROVEN] (BCHKS, eprint 2025/2055, Thm. 1.5 = ABF Thm. 4.12 core).** With $\eta=1-\sqrt\rho-\delta>0$:
$$
\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}(C,\delta)\;\le\;\frac{C_\rho\, n}{\eta^5\,|F|},\qquad\text{valid for }\delta\le1-\sqrt\rho-\eta,\quad\varepsilon^\ast=0.
$$
The $\eta$-exponent $5$ is firm (BCHKS Table 1); the leading constant $C_\rho$ is an unspecified $O_\rho(\cdot)$ — **[OUR-DERIVED caveat]** we treat it as a `VERIFY` placeholder ($C_\rho=1$ in the calculator). Linear in $n$ (vs $n^2 d\sim n^3$ for Bordage–Chiesa), hence asymptotically stronger, but kept unverified until $C_\rho$ is pinned.

**Field-size consequence [OUR-DERIVED].** The *verified* Bordage–Chiesa numerator $n^2 d$ costs $\approx 2\log_2 n+\log_2(\rho n)$ bits ($\approx60$ at $n=2^{20}$, $\approx90$ at $2^{30}$). Certifying $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}\le2^{-128}$ from this single-code bound therefore needs $\log_2|F|\gtrsim256$ for $n$ up to $2^{30}$: M31/BabyBear/Goldilocks **cannot** certify $2^{-128}$ from the single-code MCA bound at all (see `calculator/out/delta_star_tables.md`). Those deployments reach 128-bit soundness via the query term $(1-\delta)^t$ plus repetition/PoW and by running the soundness argument in a large extension — not from a single-code $2^{-128}$ MCA certificate. (BCHKS Thm 1.5's linear-$n$ bound would relax this *if* $C_\rho$ were pinned.)

$\Rightarrow$ **$\delta_{\text{known-positive}}=J=1-\sqrt\rho$** for smooth RS over all fields — **as a radius-level theorem** ($\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}\le\mathrm{poly}(n)\,d/|F|$ up to $J$). Whether that error meets the prize target $\varepsilon^\ast=2^{-128}$ is a *separate, field-size* question: per the consequence above, only $\ge256$-bit fields certify it from the single-code bound. Throughout this ledger, "positive to Johnson" at 31/64/128-bit fields means the radius-level theorem, **not** a certified $\varepsilon^\ast=2^{-128}$ lower bound.

Thus $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{ca}}=1$ reaching down to the list-decoding capacity radius $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$; $\delta^\ast_C$ is provably strictly below Singleton capacity.

\delta=(1-\rho)-\tfrac{2}{K\log n}\qquad\big(\eta=2/s=2/(K\log n)\text{ below capacity}\big)

### 2.3 The barrier: BCHKS Theorem 1.9

**[PROVEN] (BCHKS Thm. 1.9 = ABF Thm. 5.2; all RS).** For $\gamma=\mathrm{LDR}_{F,D,q}(\delta_{\min})+2/n$ (just past the list-decoding radius *for list size $q$*), there exist $f,g$ with $\ge q/(2n)$ close line points but $\Delta([f,g],C^2)\ge\delta_{\min}-1/n$, so $a/q\ge1/(2n)$ independent of $q$. **Consequence:** improving MCA / proximity gaps beyond the Johnson radius for *any* RS code **requires** first improving its list-decoding radius (at list size $q$) beyond Johnson — a separately hard, in-general-false-for-some-RS, long-open problem.

The barrier is *conditional, not absolute* (`line-decoding-analysis.md` §2.3): it does not forbid $\delta^\ast_C>J$; it proves that any *small-error* ($\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}\le\mathrm{poly}(n)/q$) certificate of $\delta^\ast_C>J$ for smooth RS is itself a list-decoding-beyond-Johnson result. The only crack is **large-error (M)CA** (error $a/q$ not $\ll1/n$ but still $\le2^{-128}$), which both CS25 and CGHLL flag as the realistic target. This is exactly why the prize poses *both* challenges. (The list-size challenge's escape from this barrier is the short paper's §4.)

## 3. The line-decoding frontier: core-bet verdict and the sub-lemma close-outs

The positive MCA route relies on **line-decodability** (ABF Def. 4.20 / GG25 Def. 3.1): $C$ is $(\delta,a,b)$-line-decodable if whenever $\ge a$ scalars $\gamma$ put $f_1+\gamma f_2$ within $\delta$ of *some* codeword $U(\gamma)$, then $\ge b$ of those codewords lie on a single line $\{u_1+\gamma u_2\}$ of codewords. **[PROVEN] (GG25 Thm. 3.5 = ABF Thm. 4.21):** if $C$ is $(\delta,a,n+1)$-line-decodable then $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}(C,\delta)\le a/|F|$. This is the entire positive route: a line-decoding theorem with threshold $a\le2^{-128}|F|$ at radius $\delta$ certifies $\delta^\ast_C\ge\delta$. The exact conjectural target is **CGHLL26 (eprint 2026/532, App. A.5, Conjecture 2):** RS over any prime-field domain is line-decodable up to the list-decoding capacity radius $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ (CGHLL's "Elias radius", stated in the large-$q$-approximation convention — §1) with threshold $a=\ell(\theta)n+o(n)$, $\ell(\theta)\le c_1 2^{c_2 H(\rho)/\eta}$ ($c_1=c_2=1$ calibrated to KK25); "the same is true if the alphabet is from an extension field of $F_p$" — originally stated without evidence, and now given its first concrete (negative-side) support: the KK25 worst case extends verbatim to genuine $F_{p^e}$ and sits *at/below* the threshold $a$ below $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$, exactly as on primes (§3.2, `n2-verdict.md`), so the extension clause is **consistent-and-tight**, not refuted.

### 3.1 Core-bet verdict

**[Assessment, from `line-decoding-analysis.md` §7]** The *strong* (up-to-MDS-capacity, small-error) core bet is **DEAD**: CS25 Cor. 1 gives $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{ca}}=1$ below $1-\rho$, and Kambiré gives explicit smooth-prime bad lines at $(1-\rho)-\Theta(1/\log n)$. The achievable ceiling is the **list-decoding capacity radius $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$**, short of MDS capacity by $\sim1/\log_2 q$. Moreover Thm 1.9 collapses the positive *small-error* MCA route into an equally hard list-decoding problem (§2.3), so the realistic positive form is **large-error** line-decodability up to $R_{\mathrm{cap}}-\Theta(1/\log_2|F|)$, with error $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}=2^{O(H(\rho)/\eta)}n/|F|$ exponential in $1/\eta$ (CGHLL §5.5: $\approx0.83\beta$–$0.91\beta$ bits/query, not the full $\beta$). The pessimistic scenario $\delta^\ast_C=J$ is **forced in char-2** (BCHKS Cor. 1.7: a bad line at $\delta=J$ with $a\ge n^{2-\epsilon}$, via subspace polynomials) but **unsupported in odd-characteristic prime fields** (no known construction puts a smooth-prime bad line in $(J,(1-\rho)-O(1/\log n))$; the subspace-polynomial mechanism has no prime analogue — CGHLL's central thesis). So the core bet, properly stated as *large-error line-decodability up to $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$*, is **likely TRUE for smooth prime RS** but **currently unproven**, reducing to the smooth-domain list-size sub-lemma P$'$ below.

### 3.2 The extension-field case is settled at ESTABLISHED-MODULO-FORMALIZATION grade: the counterexample mechanism is field-agnostic

The smooth-domain negatives are stated over prime fields (Kambiré: Linnik primes $p\equiv1\bmod n$; BCHKS Thm 1.13: prime/Mersenne; KK25 Lemma 9: multiplicative subgroup of a *prime* field), and the char-2 results (BCHKS Cor. 1.7) are the opposite extreme. The intermediate case — RS over a smooth subgroup of an *odd-characteristic extension field* $F_{p^e}$ (M31$^2$, BabyBear/Goldilocks extensions — the actually-deployed FRI fields) — was once flagged as the untouched opening. **It is now settled at EMF grade (`n2-verdict.md`, exact computation): the smooth-domain counterexample mechanism is FIELD-AGNOSTIC.** The firing condition ($e_2=0$), the bad scalar ($\lambda=e_1$), and the distinct-bad-scalar count are **characteristic-zero cyclotomic invariants** — $e_1,e_2$ are reductions mod the characteristic $p$ of fixed elements of $\mathbb{Z}[\xi_s]$, independent of the extension degree $e$ — so genuine odd-char extensions $GF(p^e)$ realize the *same* count as the prime field $GF(p)$, for primes outside the finite bad set excluded by the KK25 distinctness calibration (verified exactly, $10/10$ unsaturated cases; a decoder-free certified bad line exists on genuine $GF(31^2)$, $GF(127^2)$ at $\delta=\tfrac12,\tfrac34$). The "no additive subspace" hope does not buy extension fields any extra safety, because the obstruction here is **multiplicative** (cyclotomic subset sums), not additive, and it lives in $F_{p^e}$ identically to $F_p$.

Three consequences (all **ESTABLISHED-MODULO-FORMALIZATION**, fail-closed): (i) the negative extends to genuine odd-char extensions at the *same* constant-$\eta=2/s$ band as primes — extensions are **not** a haven; (ii) the construction is **sub-threshold at 256-bit** for every target rate (the distinct-bad-scalar count needs $s\gtrsim512$ to exceed $2^{128}$ under the rigorous binomial count — even the most optimistic characteristic-zero count needs $s\approx162$ (§2.4) — but the Kambiré distinctness/Linnik window needs $s\lesssim85$ at 256-bit — empty intersection; field-bit boundary $b^\ast\approx140$, a witness existing only for $|F|\le 2^{140}$ where the threshold degenerates), identical for prime and extension; (iii) it does **not** refute CGHLL Conj. 2's extension clause — the firing radius $\delta=\text{capacity}-2/s$ sits *below* $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ (since $\eta=2/s>1/\log_2 p$, with margin far exceeding the $\le0.0017$ large-$q$-approximation gap of §1), and the count $\approx 2^{H(2\rho)/(2\eta)}$ is at/below the conjecture's exponential threshold $a=2^{H(\rho)/\eta}n$, so the extension clause is *consistent-and-tight* exactly as the prime clause. **There is therefore no field-type opening: extensions are neither a haven nor a special vulnerability.** The genuine remaining frontier is the *positive* keystone P$'$ (§3.5). Note the asymmetry: the **negative endpoint mechanism is field-agnostic** (this section), but the positive **P$'$** route should first be proved for **prime-field multiplicative subgroups**, with the extension-field analogue stated separately (open) — P$'$ is *not* itself field-agnostic.

### 3.3 Sub-lemma N1 (extend Kambiré to $\rho=\tfrac12$) — CONDITIONAL CLOSURE modulo a named distinctness extension (KK25 Lemma 9 beyond its stated range)

> **Sub-lemma N1 (`n1-rho-half.md`).** *Over a smooth $\langle\omega\rangle\subset F_p$, $n=2^t$, the line $f=X^{rm},g=X^{(r-1)m}$ at rate exactly $\rho=\tfrac12$ ($r=s/2+2$) has $\ge n^C$ proximates at $\delta=\tfrac12-\eta$, $\eta=2/s=\Theta(1/\log n)$, yet $\Delta([f,g],C^2)>\delta$.*
> **Verdict — conditional closure modulo a named distinctness extension (KK25 Lemma 9 beyond its stated range).** The two **unconditional** halves go through *verbatim* at $\rho=\tfrac12$: (i) the degree obstruction $\deg g=(r-1)m>k$, and (ii) the structural no-CA bound $S^\ast\le(r-1)m<rm$ (both pure root-counting, rate- and field-blind). The bad-scalar count is supplied by the **PROVEN** closed form $|H^{(+r)}|=\sum_u\binom{s/2}{u}2^u=2^{\Theta(s)}$ (antipodal-pair / $\{-1,0,+1\}$-vector bijection, verified vs brute force at $s=8,16,32$). Kambiré's own count $\binom{s/2}{r}$ is **identically zero** at $\rho=\tfrac12$ (since $r=s/2+2>s/2$) and **KK25 Lemma 9 is out of range** ($r\le\varphi(m)/2=s/4$ is violated); the true count is *larger*, not absent. **Payoff (in Kambiré's $\deg\le k$ convention — under strict $\deg<k$ the construction fires not at exact rate $\tfrac12$ but at $\tfrac12+1/n$, an $O(1/n)$ slack; see the residual below):** $\delta^\ast_C(\tfrac12)\le\tfrac12-\Theta(1/\log n)$ — concretely $\le0.4375$ at 128-bit; at 256-bit **not threshold-established** (R13: count $3^{32}=2^{51}\ll2^{128}$; $0.46875$ mechanism-asymptotic reference only, power-of-two convention $s=64$ — §2.4) — at the single most-deployed rate.
> **Field-agnostic corollary.** The count is a characteristic-zero cyclotomic invariant, so genuine odd-char extensions $F_{p^e}$ give the *identical* count (25 distinct close scalars at the tested instance over $GF(p^2)$); the $\rho=\tfrac12$ endpoint extends to the deployed extension case (`n2-verdict.md` mechanism).
> **The single residual (named).** A routine, numerically-airtight-but-not-yet-formalized strengthening of KK25 Lemma 9's $\{-1,0,+1\}$-cyclotomic-independence / resultant-distinctness bound past its stated $r\le\varphi(m)/2$ range to $r=s/2+2$ — the *for-all-$n$* distinctness-mod-$p$ guarantee. The resultant criterion ($\mathrm{Res}(\Phi_s,Q)=0\Leftrightarrow$ collision, $|\mathrm{Res}|\le(2r)^{s/2}$) is verified with **zero** counterexamples across $s\in\{8,16,32\}$, but Lemma 9 *as a black box* does not cover $\rho=\tfrac12$. The result also uses Kambiré's own $\deg\le k$ convention (strict $\deg<k$ gives zero firing at rate exactly $\tfrac12$; the construction lives at rate $\tfrac12+1/n$, an $O(1/n)$ slack vanishing asymptotically).
> **Honest prize framing.** This is a **near-capacity** endpoint, *not* a closure of the open band: it forecloses only $(0.4375,\,0.5)$ at $\rho=\tfrac12$/128-bit (at 256-bit nothing is foreclosed at threshold — R13); it does **not** touch the open $(J,R_{\mathrm{cap}})$ band ($=(0.293,\,0.492)$ at 128-bit / $(0.293,\,0.496)$ at 256-bit) and is **consistent with** (does not refute) CGHLL Conj. 2 ($\delta_{\text{unsafe}}<R_{\mathrm{cap}}$, count at/below the threshold). It supplies a conditional negative endpoint at the most-deployed rate; the genuine frontier remains the positive keystone P$'$. **Never "unconditional" / "proven" tout court — it is CONDITIONAL on the named Lemma-9 extension.**

### 3.4 Sub-lemma N2 (extend Kambiré to an odd-characteristic extension field) — SETTLED (ESTABLISHED-MODULO-FORMALIZATION: field-agnostic, sub-threshold, Conj-2-consistent)

> **Sub-lemma N2.** *Originally posed as "construct an analogous bad line over a smooth subgroup $\langle\omega\rangle$ of an odd-characteristic extension field $F_{p^e}$." Settled by exact computation (`n2-verdict.md`) at EMF grade; no longer an open attack.*
> **Close-out.** The counterexample mechanism is **field-agnostic**: the distinct-bad-scalar count is a characteristic-zero cyclotomic invariant ($e_1,e_2$ are reductions mod $p$ of fixed elements of $\mathbb{Z}[\xi_s]$, independent of the extension degree $e$), so $GF(p^e)$ realizes the *same* count as $GF(p)$ (verified exactly, $10/10$ unsaturated cases; the relevant obstruction is the *multiplicative* relation among the $\zeta^i$, which behaves identically over $F_p$ and $F_{p^e}$). The bad line **does extend** structurally to genuine extensions (decoder-free certificate at $\delta=\tfrac12,\tfrac34$ over $GF(31^2),GF(127^2)$), but it is **sub-threshold at 256-bit** (count needs $s\gtrsim512$ to exceed $2^{128}$, distinctness needs $s\lesssim85$ — empty window; boundary $b^\ast\approx140$) and it does **not** refute CGHLL Conj. 2's extension clause ($\delta=\text{capacity}-2/s<R_{\mathrm{cap}}$, count $\approx 2^{H(2\rho)/(2\eta)}$ at/below the threshold $a=2^{H(\rho)/\eta}n$). **Net: extensions are neither a haven nor a special vulnerability — N2 is settled at EMF grade, field-agnostic.**

### 3.5 The positive keystone P$'$ and the recommended priority

> **Sub-lemma P$'$ (smooth-domain small list just beyond Johnson — the positive keystone).** *For $L=\langle\omega\rangle\subset F_p$, $|L|=n$, rate $\rho$, there is $\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_0(\rho)>0$ with*
> $$|\Lambda(\mathrm{RS}[F_p,L,\rho n],\,J+\varepsilon_0)|\;\le\;O_\rho(1/\varepsilon_0)\quad(\text{constant in }n).$$
> **Conclusion / payoff:** via the GG25 Lemma-8 bootstrap (CGHLL A.5) this supplies line-decodability, hence $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}(\mathrm{RS}[F_p,L,\rho n],J+\varepsilon_0)\le a(n)/|F|\le2^{-128}$ — the **first provable smooth-domain MCA point strictly beyond Johnson**. It is *weaker than CGHLL Conj. 1* (only $\varepsilon_0$ beyond $J$, not all the way to $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$), dodging the hardest part of the barrier.
> **Field scope (not field-agnostic).** P$'$ as stated is a **prime-field multiplicative-subgroup** statement ($L=\langle\omega\rangle\subset F_p^\times$): the char-2 analogue is *false at Johnson* (BCHKS Cor. 1.7, subspace polynomials), and the genuine odd-char **extension-field** analogue is **OPEN** (extensions contain $F_p$-subspaces, so the "no additive subspaces" lever the prime route relies on is unavailable) — it must be stated separately. So, unlike the negative endpoint mechanism (which *is* field-agnostic, §3.2), P$'$ is *not* field-agnostic.

**Recommended priority** (`line-decoding-analysis.md` §7): (1) **P$'$ first** — the positive keystone (a **prime-field multiplicative-subgroup** statement; *not* field-agnostic — its extension-field analogue is a separate, open problem), now the *single* genuine open attack: it simultaneously advances the list-decoding challenge and unlocks the MCA line-decoding theorem. (N1 carries a **conditional closure modulo the named KK25 Lemma-9 extension** (`n1-rho-half.md`) — the negative $\rho=\tfrac12$ endpoint; N2 is **settled at EMF grade**, field-agnostic — neither is a remaining open attack.) A first target band for P$'$: $\delta=J+\varepsilon_0$ for the smallest $\varepsilon_0$ beating Johnson, at $\rho=\tfrac12$, M31 (the prime-field case); the genuine odd-char extension analogue is stated separately and remains open.

## 5. Experimental corroboration

Workstream C (`experiments/small_rs_atlas/`) is **exact full-codeword enumeration** over small fields ($q\le257$, $n\le256$) — *corroborative, not cryptographic-scale*. Three findings bear on the analysis above.

**(a) Smooth $\approx$ random/full everywhere [EXPERIMENTAL].** Across 144 matched subgroup-vs-random cells, the smooth$-$random differential in mean close-count per line is $+0.037$ (std $0.207$) and in bad-line fraction is $-0.0014$ (std $0.021$) — centered on zero, bounded by sampling noise. Smooth subgroups are statistically indistinguishable from random domains; structured inputs (rational, degree-$k{+}1$) produce *fewer* bad lines than uniform-random, not more. This supports the positive / derandomization route (§3.1).

**(b) Onsets [EXPERIMENTAL].** Bad lines (high close-count, no CA) onset essentially *exactly at Johnson* (mean onset $-$ Johnson: subgroup $-0.001$, random $-0.005$), identically across domain types. But **interleaved lists stay singleton far past Johnson**: the max interleaved list size is exactly $1$ from below Johnson, through Johnson, departing from $1$ only at $\approx$ capacity $-O(1/n)$ (the `interleaved_onset.csv` data give onset $-$ capacity $\approx -0.05$ to $-0.10$, i.e. $c\approx1$–$2$ in capacity $-c/n$) and exploding *at* capacity. $m=3$ matches $m=2$ exactly. This directly corroborates the sub-problem-2 benign-list story (short paper §3): the dangerous radius for interleaved lists is capacity $-O(1/n)$, not Johnson.

**(c) The Kambiré mechanism is a genuine smooth-specific algebraic excess — near capacity only [EXPERIMENTAL].** Reproducing the monomial line $f=X^a,g=X^b$ on a smooth prime subgroup: the cleanest cell is **$p=257$, $n=64$, $k=2$, $\delta=0.90625$ (= capacity $-0.0625$, capacity $=0.9688$): SMOOTH close-count $=32=$ predicted sumset size, vs matched RANDOM $=7$** (`counterexample_kambire.csv`, smooth row at $\delta\!=\!0.90625$ has `close_count=32, predicted_sumset_size=32`; random row has `close_count=7`). This is a real smooth-specific excess corroborating the sumset mechanism — but **near capacity only**; below capacity $-O(1/\log n)$ no smooth-specific bad line appears.

**Caveats [stated honestly].** (1) Field sizes are tiny ($q\le257$); the $1/|F|$ floor is huge, so $2^{-128}$-scale effects are invisible — these are *structural* statements, not quantitative bounds at cryptographic scale. (2) Sampling over words/lines/targets (each distance/list exact); a rare structured configuration could be missed. (3) **Crucially, the tested "extension" domains in *this* Wave-1 battery were $\mathrm{GF}(2^m)$ = char-2**, which has its *own* known negatives (subspace polynomials) — **NOT odd-characteristic $F_{p^e}$**. The odd-char extension case (N2) is **not** probed by this Wave-1 battery; it is settled separately by the exact cyclotomic computation of `n2-verdict.md` (§3.2, EMF grade), which establishes the counterexample mechanism is **field-agnostic** — genuine $F_{p^e}$ behaves exactly like $F_p$, sub-threshold at 256-bit, Conj-2-consistent. (4) The per-line common-agreement maximum $S^\ast$ is an exact proxy for CA on a line, not the protocol's $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mca}}$; bridging the two is the theory step.

## 7. What this ledger does and does not establish

- **The companion short paper establishes** (rigorous, from cited Johnson + Elias bounds) the **proven two-sided bracket $\delta^\ast_C{}^{(2)}\in[J-o(1),\,R_{\mathrm{cap}}]$ for the interleaved list-size grand challenge at $|F|>2^{128}$** (proven floor $J-\eta_{\min}$ with $\eta_{\min}=1/(2\rho B^{1/m})\le2^{-29}$ deployed, written $J-o(1)$; proven ceiling the inverse-entropy crossing $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$; e.g. $[0.293,0.496]$ at $\rho=\tfrac12$, 256-bit), the collapse to the UD supremum $(1-\rho)/2$ at $|F|=2^{128}$ (endpoint not attained — discreteness) and vacuity below — and, most importantly, that this bracket **uses no new list-decoding and therefore dodges the Thm 1.9 barrier**, demonstrating that the two grand challenges are not equivalent and that sub-problem 2 is decoupled from Thm. 1.9 with a standard list-bound bracket, while sub-problem 1 stays barrier-gated. The upper reach to $\approx R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ is **conjectural** (it requires an unproven worst-case large-list smooth-RS list-decoding theorem beyond Johnson; ABF §7.9 / P$'$), not established anywhere in this work.
- **This ledger does record (ASSEMBLED, with named soft spots)** the assembled per-field MCA negative-ceiling **assessment** $\delta^\ast_C\le(1-\rho)-2/s_{\max}(b)$ — at $\rho=\tfrac12$: $0.375$ at 31- and 64-bit, $0.4375$ at 128-bit (§2.2 Lemma, R13; KK25 cited not re-proved, $\rho=\tfrac12$ N1-conditional) — including the **withdrawal of the M31 "almost closed" reading** (honest window $[0.293,\,0.375]$, not $[0.293,\,0.306]$ — a radius-level window, §2.4: sub-256-bit fields carry no $2^{-128}$ positive certificate; M31 row read as 31-bit characteristic / 62-bit alphabet, §2.2); and it records that at **256-bit no threshold-established negative ceiling below $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ — i.e., no Kambiré-type near-capacity, constant-$\eta$ ceiling — exists from any known mechanism** (the generic CS25/Elias ceiling at $\approx R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ stands at every field size), with both rescue paths assessed (per-prime certification blocked at $\sim2^{77}$ enumeration; BGK-type growth $\approx113$ bits short) and the feasibility boundary $b^\ast\approx140$ bits (§2.4).
- **Neither artifact claims** any solution to the **MCA grand challenge**. For sub-problem 1, only the *bracket* $[J,\,R_{\mathrm{cap}})$ is known (bounded above near capacity by Kambiré/CS25/BCHKS, the negative mechanism established field-agnostic at EMF grade — the near-capacity negative holds for genuine odd-char extensions at the same band as prime, `n2-verdict.md`); a positive MCA point strictly above $J$ for smooth RS is unproven and is exactly the prize.
- **What remains for the full prize:** (1) **MCA, positive (the genuine frontier):** prove sub-lemma P$'$ (small smooth-domain list just past $J$) $\Rightarrow$ via GG25 bootstrap, the first $\delta^\ast_C>J$; the realistic ceiling is large-error line-decodability to $R_{\mathrm{cap}}-\Theta(1/\log_2|F|)$ (CGHLL Conj. 2), unproven for structured domains. **P$'$ should first be proved for prime-field multiplicative subgroups; its extension-field analogue is stated separately and is open — P$'$ is *not* field-agnostic** (only the negative endpoint mechanism is). (2) **MCA, negative:** the $\rho=\tfrac12$ endpoint now carries a **conditional closure modulo a named distinctness extension (KK25 Lemma 9 beyond its stated range)** — sub-lemma N1 ($\delta^\ast_C(\tfrac12)\le\tfrac12-\Theta(1/\log n)$, prime and $F_{p^e}$, **in Kambiré's $\deg\le k$ convention**: under strict $\deg<k$ the construction fires at rate $\tfrac12+1/n$, not exact $\tfrac12$ — §3.3); it forecloses only the near-capacity band, not the open $(J,R_{\mathrm{cap}})$. (N2, the odd-char extension negative, is **settled at EMF grade**: field-agnostic, sub-threshold at 256-bit, Conj-2-consistent — `n2-verdict.md`; no longer an open attack.) The genuinely open negative item is the **256-bit ceiling below $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$** (Kambiré-type near-capacity, constant-$\eta$): no known mechanism reaches the $2^{-128}$ threshold below $R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ at $b=256$ (the generic CS25/Elias ceiling at $\approx R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ stands) — per-prime certification blocked at $\sim2^{77}$ enumeration, BGK-type growth $\approx113$ bits short (§2.4) — with feasibility boundary $b^\ast\approx140$ bits. (3) **List-size, upper reach:** the smooth-domain interleaved-list conjecture (CONJECTURED; ABF §7.9) — it governs only the conjectural reach to $\approx R_{\mathrm{cap}}$ for $|F|>2^{128}$; the knife-edge $|F|=2^{128}$ *value* is pinned at the UD supremum (short paper Prop. (B)) and is not open.

Structural work toward the positive side is ongoing.
